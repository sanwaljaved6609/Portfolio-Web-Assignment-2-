Favourite Personalities

01. Hazrat Muhammad S.A.W

Hazrat Muhammad (SAW) is the founder of Islam and the last prophet of Allah. He was born in Mecca in 570 CE and began receiving revelations from Allah at the age of 40. He spent the rest of his life spreading the message of Islam and the worship of Allah. He is highly respected around the world for his wisdom, kindness, and devotion to Allah. His teachings, recorded in the holy book of Islam, the Quran, continue to guide and inspire millions of people today.

02. Dr. Allama Mohammad Iqbal

Allama Iqbal was a renowned poet, philosopher, and politician who is considered one of the most influential figures in South Asian history. He was born in Sialkot, Pakistan in 1877 and received his education in Lahore and Cambridge, England. Iqbal is widely recognized as the poet of the East and his poetry has been translated into several languages. He is also known for his political activism, particularly in advocating for the creation of a separate Muslim state, which ultimately led to the formation of Pakistan in 1947. Iqbal's ideas and philosophy continue to inspire people in Pakistan and around the world.

03. Quaid-e-Azam Mohammad Ali Jinnah

Quaid-e-Azam, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, was the founder of Pakistan and is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in the country's history. He was born in Karachi in 1876 and received his early education in Sindh before moving to England to study law. Jinnah was a gifted lawyer and political leader who played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement. He worked tirelessly to create a separate Muslim state in the Indian subcontinent, which eventually led to the formation of Pakistan in 1947. Jinnah served as Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death in 1948. He is still remembered as a visionary leader and a symbol of Pakistan's struggle for independence.

04. Imran Khan

Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and former Prime Minister of Pakistan. He was born in Lahore in 1952 and attended Oxford University, where he studied politics and economics. Khan gained international recognition as the captain of the Pakistani cricket team, leading them to their first-ever World Cup victory in 1992. After retiring from cricket, Khan entered politics and founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party in 1996. He ran unsuccessfully in several elections before leading his party to victory in the 2018 general elections and becoming Prime Minister. Khan's political vision focuses on fighting corruption, improving education, and promoting economic growth in Pakistan. He is also a strong advocate for environmental conservation and has launched several initiatives to protect Pakistan's natural resources.
